What is the result of merging two branches together? Why do we represent it in
the diagram the way we do?
The result is a branch with the commits of both branches; we represent it in that way to show where the merge was done from.

What are the pros and cons of Git's automatic merging vs. always doing merges
manually?
Pros: Easily make big changes that are totally new and won't present a problem.
Cons: Merge conflicts could lead to a very weird chunk of code which might not even compile

Doing manual merges is useful only when a merge conflict situation occurs
